# Quarantine report — `ideas3(8).txt`

These proposals are preserved for possible research use but are not canonical graph facts.

## Type-III AQFT recovery

A finite-dimensional conditional-mutual-information recovery theorem does not automatically extend to local type-III algebras. Promotion requires a precise split inclusion or operator-algebraic entropy/recovery theorem, with constants and domains.

**Falsifier:** a required entropy or fidelity quantity is not well-defined or no uniform recovery channel exists in the chosen operator-algebraic setting.

## Everett/Deutsch branch recovery

The branch-register construction is an interpretive model. It does not establish the many-worlds interpretation, inter-branch communication or recoherence of macroscopic records.

**Falsifier:** no branch-independent channel preserves coherent superpositions and reference entanglement across the proposed code.

## Schur geometry of holes

Interpreting the modified polymer metric as a minimum-spanning-tree metric after eliminating hole variables is plausible but unproved. Schur complements can create dense boundary couplings.

**Falsifier:** a finite example where eliminating a hole creates an effective edge whose cost is not controlled by the modified metric.

## Mixed hard/soft forest expansion

A single colored forest combining polymer incompatibility edges and residual Gaussian covariance edges could avoid paying two tree entropies, but no identity with the composed Gaussian-localization/Mayer/Ursell construction is supplied.

**Falsifier:** coefficient comparison fails already for three polymers or loses the exact target union.

## Variational decay allocation and stopping scale

Optimizing the split between target decay and child moments is mathematically meaningful only after the moment constant \(M_d(\lambda)\) is defined and controlled. The proposed physical stopping scale additionally needs a calibrated continuum trajectory.

**Falsifier:** \(M_d(\lambda)\) grows too rapidly for the variational envelope to improve any admissible window.

## BV/BRST application to the Yang–Mills RG producer

The projector-commutator identity is formal, but the claim that it yields a small Yang–Mills boundary activity remains speculative.

**Falsifier:** the concrete homotopy is nonlocal, the commutator is not boundary-supported, or its polymer norm is no smaller than the original activity.

## Arithmetic RG programmes for the Riemann hypothesis

The proposed prime-layer Schur recursion, Catalan self-energy closure, Stieltjes programme and arithmetic reflection-positivity analogy are research programmes, not proofs. Several displayed formulae are schematic and depend on sign, domain and canonical-product conventions.

**Falsifier:** a finite truncated Weil matrix violates the proposed coercivity budget, or the claimed Stieltjes equivalence fails under the exact growth and pole hypotheses.

## Remote preparation versus teleportation

Reeh–Schlieder gives qualitative approximation by local operators, not a state-independent high-probability teleportation protocol. Operator norms, energy and success probability can deteriorate severely.

**Falsifier:** no uniform channel with nonvanishing controlled success probability works over the full code space.

## Bell and cosmological overreach

Bell violation, vacuum nonfactorization and no-signalling are distinct statements. They do not prove that every particle pair is entangled, that correlations remain finite at infinite separation, or that extra dimensions and portals exist.

**Falsifier:** the proposed conclusion does not follow from the exact algebraic assumptions after removing interpretive language.
