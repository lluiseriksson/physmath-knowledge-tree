## Source-required or speculative refinements

### Relative BV–RG retract

The proposed identity \(Qh+hQ=\mathrm{id}-\Pi\) could isolate a cohomological remainder, but no concrete local homotopy, projection or RG-compatible bound is supplied for the actual gauge activity.

### Mayer–Ward transgression

A nonlinear Ward identity across Mayer factors may preserve cancellation before triangle inequalities. It needs a finite theorem showing that the remainder retains support and modified-metric decay.

### Berezin matrix-tree encoding

A fermionic representation is useful only if it preserves the exact union, root, incompatibility edges and positive majorant. A determinant that reproduces a global tree sum but erases \(Y\) does not solve the target-sensitive problem.

### Parent-dependent kernel and spectral radius

Replacing \(4C_2^2\) by the spectral radius of a local incompatibility kernel is plausible, but no positive operator or contraction theorem is supplied.

### Dimension laboratory

The quoted thresholds for dimensions two through five are diagnostic numbers, not physical constants, and lack the exact reproducible formula in this source.

### Direct covariance route

A direct influence-decay proof might bypass part of the \(H^\#\) packaging, but it must still construct the physical gauge operator, large-field control and the R-operation.

### Space–scale polymers and root optimizations

Multiscale carriers, active-cardinality tilts, root averaging and restricted tree families may improve constants later. They should not precede the baseline exact-target leaf theorem.
