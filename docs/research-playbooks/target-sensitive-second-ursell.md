# Target-sensitive Appendix-F leaf summation

## Purpose

This playbook records a reusable proof discipline for second-Ursell cluster bounds. It is not a claim that the Yang-Mills source hypotheses are already proved.

## Failure mode

A root-marked free sum may factor as

\[
V(r,n)=\operatorname{rootedMass}(r)\operatorname{totalMass}^n.
\]

The exact target, tree edges and parent-child incompatibility have disappeared. Such a quantity cannot manufacture uniform decay in \(d_M(Y)\).

## Invariants to retain

1. exact union \(\bigcup_iX_i=Y\);
2. a spanning incompatibility tree;
3. a marked root in `skeleton(Y)`;
4. parent-child incompatibility after orientation;
5. the shifted metric \(m(X)=d_M(X)+1\).

## Safe order

1. Split the one-polymer weight into residual target decay and leaf-entropy reserve.
2. Apply metric stitching while the exact union and tree remain visible.
3. Extract \(e^{-\rho m(Y)}\).
4. Root and orient the tree.
5. Eliminate deepest leaves with parent-dependent factorial moments.
6. Apply the rooted moment at the final vertex.
7. Obtain \(C_2^{2n+1}\prod_v\operatorname{childCount}(v)!\) for one tree.
8. Sum tree shapes with audited marker and factorial normalization.
9. Insert \(\varepsilon^{n+1}\) from the real first activity.
10. Sum the geometric series only after verifying \(4C_2^2\varepsilon<1\).

## Rate ledger

\[
(\kappa-\kappa_0-2)-(\kappa-3\kappa_0-3)=2\kappa_0+1.
\]

Track where every unit is spent. Do not hide active-cardinality tilts, marker costs or polynomial factors inside an unnamed constant.

## Alternative route

A direct producer for the complete Appendix-F cluster theorem is acceptable if it identifies the current objects with the source objects and yields the same residual profile. Do not add another contract equivalent to the missing estimate.

## Minimal falsifier

For \(n\le3\), enumerate rooted trees and finite polymer assignments. Compare the exact-union tree sum, the free marked sum, bottom-up elimination and the proposed child-factorial bound. Stop if a counterexample exposes volume growth or target loss.

## Parent normalization

When a child moment bound ends with a factor of the parent shifted metric, divide the child kernel by that positive parent weight. The product of all denominators is recovered exactly from rooted child multiplicities. Prove the parent-product identity before applying a vertexwise walk theorem; never replace it by an unpinned global mass.

## Exact Catalan closure

After obtaining the fixed-tree coefficient

\[
M^{2n+1}\prod_v \operatorname{childCount}(v)!,
\]

audit whether the tree class is the complete set of labelled trees rooted at a fixed label. In that convention,

\[
\frac{n+1}{(n+1)!}
\sum_T\prod_v\operatorname{childCount}_T(v)!
=\operatorname{Cat}_n.
\]

Use the exact Catalan generating function when available. Retain the `4^n` theorem as a robust fallback and as a comparison test; do not change the analytic constants until the exact enumeration is checked against the consuming definition.
