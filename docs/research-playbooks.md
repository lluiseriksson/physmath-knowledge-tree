# Research Playbooks

These playbooks are starting points, not claims of solution.

## Riemann Hypothesis

Useful moves:

- `spectralize`: search for a self-adjoint operator or trace formula shadow.
- `randomize`: compare zero statistics with random matrix ensembles.
- `categorify`: look for sheaf/cohomology replacements of analytic objects.
- `noncommutativize`: test whether missing geometry lives in a noncommutative
  space.

First Lean targets:

- zeta definitions and elementary analytic statements,
- finite Euler products,
- toy explicit formulas over finite structures.

## Yang-Mills Mass Gap

Useful moves:

- `construct`: define measures or lattice approximations.
- `represent`: use compact Lie groups and representation theory.
- `renormalize`: track scale flow and relevant observables.
- `spectralize`: express mass gap as a spectral gap statement.

First Lean targets:

- compact groups,
- gauge-action toy models,
- finite lattice analogues.

## Navier-Stokes

Useful moves:

- `scale`: isolate critical norms.
- `local_to_global`: connect local regularity to global continuation.
- `probabilize`: compare deterministic cascades with stochastic models.
- `renormalize`: track energy transfer across scales.

First Lean targets:

- weak formulations,
- energy inequalities in simplified spaces,
- scale-invariant toy estimates.

## Hodge Conjecture

Useful moves:

- `motivic_lift`: translate cycles through motives and periods.
- `p_adic_shadow`: compare complex and p-adic cohomological data.
- `categorify`: use derived categories and functorial filtrations.

First Lean targets:

- cohomology abstractions,
- algebraic cycles as data types,
- toy projective varieties.

## Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer

Useful moves:

- `l_function_bridge`: connect analytic rank and algebraic rank.
- `modular_transfer`: use modularity and automorphic forms.
- `iwasawa_deform`: study families instead of isolated curves.

First Lean targets:

- elliptic curve basics,
- finite group ranks,
- toy L-series.

## P vs NP

Useful moves:

- `algebrize`: translate Boolean complexity into polynomial complexity.
- `geometrize`: study circuit classes as varieties or orbit closures.
- `proof_complexity`: attack barriers through proof systems.
- `categorify`: track reductions and resources functorially.

First Lean targets:

- reductions,
- finite languages,
- simple circuit models.

## Poincare Conjecture as calibration

This problem is solved. Keep it as a calibration board:

- Does the graph recover topology + geometry + flow?
- Can agents identify why Ricci flow mattered?
- Can the repo represent a solved proof strategy without pretending to
  reconstruct every detail?
