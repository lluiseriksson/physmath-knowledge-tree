# Code of Conduct

We want this project to be a rigorous, welcoming place to build educational software.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and constructive.
- Critique ideas and evidence, not people.
- Welcome questions and different levels of subject expertise.
- Credit sources and contributors appropriately.
- Correct mathematical, scientific, translation, or accessibility errors without ridicule.
- Protect private information and report security concerns through the documented channel.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized conduct, deliberate misinformation, doxxing, spam, and sustained disruption are not tolerated in project spaces.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove content, close discussions, reject contributions, or restrict participation when necessary to protect the community. Serious or repeated incidents should be reported privately through GitHub's repository contact or security-advisory mechanism. Reports will be reviewed as promptly and confidentially as practical.

This policy applies to issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and other spaces representing the project.
